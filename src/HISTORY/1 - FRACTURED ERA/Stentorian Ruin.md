The Stentorian Ruin, or the Stentorian Period is the first defining event of modern history or post-[[Ancient History]], marking the beginning of the [[Fractured Era]]. The Ruin was an event that broke up [[Mirus]] into its modern [[Globule]]. There is no information about what caused it or how Aeden broke into modern Nebula.

## The Ruin

Very little is known for certain about the causes of the Catastophe, however certain things are common knowledge:

* The Stentorian broke Nebel into many pieces known as plates.
* These plates did not collapse onto themselves because immediately [[Formatite]] filled the space, holding the plates together
* People survived despite the cataclysm and recorded days.
* The Cataclysm caused dust to form everywhere, making the land unable to be seen for [[Stentorian Ruin#The 40 days|40 days]].
* For those 40 days, the land also ringed in a tone for the entire duration

## Historical placement

The Stentorian happened in 0 BS, on the first half of the month of August. The January of the next year is declared as a standard year 1 AS

#### As a landmark date

For fossil and historical records the terms BR (Before Ruin) and AS (After Ruin) are utilized to define the division of the Stentorian in history. Due to the year being very well recorded.

## Evidence

Most written records of the Stentorian have disappeared with time, oral tradition warping what happened or records having been missing since. Though evidence of an event does exist and is researched by paleontologists, archaeologists and general historians.

### Paleontological

Most compelling evidence of the world once being a singular plate is the fact that fossil records of multiple species have been found across [[Catastrophe Zones]], most notably was the comparison of the fossil of the lithosaur from the genus *Ammosaurus*, first discovered in the year 2899 in [[Apihia]], the lithosaur was assumed to have been present only in that plate, however in 2964, new evidence of another fossil in the neighboring plate [[Ametrie]] was found, the full set having been found by combining fossils from the plates. 

A secondary case, involving [[Makro]] and [[Rosalia]], where *Carnitte* fossils were found in the Sapientia sea and the Gulf of Canthara.

### Archaeological

Most of the pre-Stentorian records were found in Rose script stellae. Which like fossils, had interplate coherent appearances. Most notably in [[Apihia]], Ametrie and [[Agia]], the Rosal Plates. The large quantity of written material indicated that the pre-Stentorian civilization might've been quite advanced, with agricultural and architectural stability strong enough to survive the Stentorian. But since no true translation exists, actual evidence of it being anything more than city dwellers.

## The 40 days

The Stentorian reportedly caused the air to ring in The 40 days or the "Quarema" were a period of exactly 40 days which followed the Ruin, the sound of the event was declared to have ringed for that period.
