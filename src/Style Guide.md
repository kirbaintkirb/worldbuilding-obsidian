## The name of the world is Mirus

This document contains important details about Mirus. Writing for Mirus has a few rules:

## Macro scale:

- The main conflict is one of the spiritual and the physical
	- Spirituality is immanent and not disconnected from earthly matters, that does not mean it is physical, but instead social
	* The spiritual does affect the material because of that, things that happen here, happen here not in an ethereal plane
	* History, geography, maths and language are all immanent

* Hopelessness rules inside of alienation, that alienation separates everyone from everything creating dissociation.
	* Alienation of any kind contributes from this.
	* The beginning of the separation of humanity from the world started the [[Stentorian Ruin]].
	* This dissociation manifests as the literal division of the Globules, which warps any sense (Aeden was round, Mirus is shattered)
		* Connection if done correctly can open pathways through the haze of alienation

- Hope is not action, it is inaction, it's waiting for something to happen rather than doing it

* To the cartographers, Mirus is a belt of flat earths, a bunch of broken shapes in order, to us Mirus is the words you are currently reading

* The common form of the state is one where religion and law are **NOT** separate. There is no distinction between church and state in most cases.
	* This does not mean however that you can't practice religion outside of the state process or that you can't participate in the state process without religion (see [[Rotatorism]])

* This is an industrialized world, [[City-State of Villuvi]] was the one who began the process.

* The preferred time of writing is the "Cold War" or the [[Clean Wars]], where Still-leaning [[Telemach]] and Rotation-leaning [[Krahvkond]] try to achieve majoritary influence across the globe.
	* The Clean Wars started at the end of the [[Yearly War]] where Villuvi got bombarded by an unknown event (a la Tunguska Event)
	* 
### Physical scale:

* Mirus is a world.
	* The world is oddly shaped and broken due to an event called the [[Stentorian Ruin]]
		* At some point in the past it was called Aeden, and it was thought that it was shaped like a bell.
	* In what was the old south pole of the planet a huge pillar existed.

* Globules are the "continental" divisions, there are many.
	* Globules are slightly curved but they do not follow a curve, they have positions that are different in relation to each other.
	* There are 7 globules, but presumably there are milions
	* Six of them are inhabited and have travel:
		* Rosalia 
		* Apihia 
		* Makro 
		* Ametrie 
		* Kasha 
		* Agia
	* One is an icy wasteland: Poles
	* You travel between globules by "going up" and "going down"
		* The order of travel when going down is as follows: Ametrie -> Agia -> Rosalia -> Apihia -> Makro -> Apihia
		* Poles does not follow the order
		* There is an old pathway between Ametrie and Apihia, now inaccessible to most ways
		* "up" and "down" are only metaphors, the travel does not actually involve direction
	
* Between each plate are the [[Catastrophe Zones]], also known as Black Seas
	* They are the division of the plates and contain Lacuna, a mysterious substance that has kept the world from being completely destroyed.
		* The lacuna is odorless, tasteless, untouchable and almost colorless, in high quantities it appears to darken. It has buoyancy but with intense variations of gravity inside the zones
			* Trying to store it will give you inert coal the moment it enters a plate.
			* It grows 1 cm in every direction every year
			* The lacuna is also called arroyo in certain places
		* The lacuna lacks direction, there is no up and down, getting lost is easy
			* The method of travel is done through the [[Curved Roads]], specific fixed direction that guarantees the travel. 

### Historical scale

* After the Stentorian Ruin, the world began anew in Apihia, the first civilization lived there
	* The Apihians were able to travel to Ametrie via the Old Straight Road
	* The Apihians disappeared mysteriously one day, in a great drought
